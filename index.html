Luxembourg Embassy Facts & Information

1945
Embassy Establishment

The Embassy of Luxembourg was established shortly after World War II, strengthening diplomatic relations and providing consular services to Luxembourg citizens abroad and foreign nationals seeking to visit Luxembourg.

1957
Founding EU Member

Luxembourg was one of the six founding members of the European Economic Community, which later became the European Union. The embassy plays a crucial role in representing Luxembourg's interests within the EU framework.

1985
Schengen Agreement

The Schengen Agreement was signed aboard the ship Princess Marie-Astrid on the Moselle River near Schengen, Luxembourg. This landmark agreement abolished border controls between participating European countries.

2023
Modern Consular Services

The embassy now offers fully digital visa applications and online appointment systems, making the process more efficient for applicants while maintaining the highest standards of security and service.
